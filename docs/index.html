Abstract

LiDAR semantic segmentation degrades in adverse weather because refraction, scattering, and point dropouts corrupt geometry. Prior work, including weather simulation, mixing-based augmentation, domain randomization, and uncertainty or boundary regularization, improves robustness but still overlooks structural vulnerabilities near boundaries, corners, and sparse regions. We present a Light Geometry-aware adapter. The module aligns azimuth and applies horizontal circular padding to preserve neighbor continuity across the 0‚ó¶-360‚ó¶ wrap-around boundary. A local-window K-Nearest Neighbors gathers nearby points and computes simple local statistics, which are compressed into compact geometry-aware cues. During training, these cues drive region-aware regularization that stabilizes predictions in structurally fragile areas. The adapter is plug-and-play, complements augmentation, and can be enabled only during training with negligible inference cost. We adopt a source-only cross-weather setup where models train on SemanticKITTI and are evaluated on SemanticSTF without target labels or fine-tuning. The adapter improves mIoU by +3.4% over the data-centric augmentation baseline and by +0.3% over the class-centric regularization baseline. These results indicate that geometry-driven regularization is a key direction for all-weather LiDAR segmentation.

Limitations & Related Work

Limitations

In the highlighted regions, data-centric augmentation perturbs point locations and density in a class-agnostic way, which disproportionately harms boundary and corner points that already have few returns. As a result, nearest-neighbor relations become unreliable: points that should belong to the same object are separated, while points from different surfaces are artificially brought together, causing boundary leakage and inconsistent local topology. These geometry-driven errors lead to fragmented structures and label swaps in sparse zones, so we introduce geometry-aware regularization to maintain local consistency under corruption.

Related Work

(a) Standard LiDAR segmentation pipelines focus on feature learning after preprocessing (voxelization, projection, sampling), assuming neighbor structure is reliable. (b) Data-centric augmentation extends this by simulating corruption through selective jittering and point dropping, but the supervision still comes only from the segmentation objective, so geometry-sensitive errors remain, especially where local neighborhoods are fragile. (c) We explicitly model local geometry and use it as a training-time signal to regularize predictions and control point drop, improving robustness under source-only cross-weather evaluation.
